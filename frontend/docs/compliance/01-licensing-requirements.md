## Federal Requirements

### 1. FinCEN MSB Registration

**Requirement**: Register as a Money Service Business with the Financial Crimes Enforcement Network (FinCEN)

**Key Details**:
- **Form**: FinCEN Form 107
- **Timeline**: Must register within 180 days of starting operations
- **Renewal**: Every 2 years
- **Cost**: No registration fee
- **Processing Time**: 30-60 days

**Registration Process**:
1. Obtain an Employer Identification Number (EIN)
2. Create BSA E-Filing account
3. Complete Form 107 with:
   - Legal business name and DBAs
   - Business addresses
   - Owner information
   - MSB activities (money transmission, currency exchange)
4. Submit electronically
5. Receive MSB registration number

**Obligations After Registration**:
- Implement AML program
- File Currency Transaction Reports (CTRs)
- File Suspicious Activity Reports (SARs)
- Maintain records for 5 years
- Conduct independent compliance reviews

### 2. IRS Requirements

- Obtain EIN
- File Form 8300 for cash transactions over $10,000
- Report cryptocurrency transactions on Form 1099-K
- Comply with backup withholding requirements

### 3. SEC Considerations

While not directly required for basic crypto exchange operations, consider:
- Ensure tokens traded are not securities
- Avoid offering services that would require broker-dealer registration
- Monitor evolving regulations

## State Licensing Requirements

### Money Transmitter License (MTL) Overview

Most states require cryptocurrency exchanges to obtain a Money Transmitter License. Requirements vary significantly by state.

### State-by-State Requirements

#### Tier 1: Priority States (Most Crypto-Friendly)

**Wyoming**
- **License**: Special Purpose Depository Institution (SPDI) or Exemption
- **Requirements**: Minimal for crypto-to-crypto; MTL for fiat services
- **Bond**: $0 for SPDI
- **Timeline**: 3-6 months
- **Annual Fee**: $5,000

**Texas**
- **License**: Money Transmitter License
- **Requirements**: 
  - $1 million net worth
  - Audited financials
  - Surety bond: $300,000 - $2 million
- **Timeline**: 6-9 months
- **Annual Fee**: $2,500

**Florida**
- **License**: Money Services Business License
- **Requirements**:
  - Net worth: $100,000 - $2 million (based on volume)
  - Surety bond: $50,000 - $2 million
- **Timeline**: 6-12 months
- **Annual Fee**: $575

#### Tier 2: Major Markets

**New York**
- **License**: BitLicense + Money Transmitter License
- **Requirements**:
  - Extensive application (150+ pages)
  - Capital requirements based on risk assessment
  - Comprehensive compliance program
  - Surety bond: Varies
- **Timeline**: 18-24 months
- **Application Fee**: $5,000 (non-refundable)
- **Annual Assessment**: Based on volume

**California**
- **License**: Money Transmitter License
- **Requirements**:
  - Net worth: $500,000+
  - Surety bond: $250,000 - $7 million
  - Audited financials
- **Timeline**: 12-18 months
- **Application Fee**: $5,000
- **Annual Fee**: $2,500

#### Tier 3: Additional States

**Illinois**
- **License**: Money Transmitter License (TOMA)
- **Requirements**:
  - Net worth: $100,000
  - Surety bond: $100,000 - $2 million
- **Timeline**: 6-9 months
- **Annual Fee**: $3,000

**Washington**
- **License**: Money Transmitter License
- **Requirements**:
  - Net worth: $10,000 - $1 million
  - Surety bond: $10,000 - $550,000
- **Timeline**: 6-9 months
- **Annual Fee**: $2,000

### States Without MTL Requirements for Crypto

As of 2025, these states have exemptions or no requirements:
- **Montana**: No MTL requirement
- **New Hampshire**: Exemption for virtual currency
- **Rhode Island**: Limited exemption
- **Tennessee**: Exemption for crypto-to-crypto

### Prohibited States

Some states have restrictive requirements or effective bans:
- **Hawaii**: Requires 1:1 cash reserves (effectively prohibitive)
- **Alaska**: Unclear regulations, high risk

## Maintenance and Renewals

### Annual Requirements

1. **License Renewals**
   - Submit renewal applications 60-90 days before expiration
   - Update financial statements
   - Pay renewal fees

2. **Reporting Obligations**
   - Quarterly financial reports
   - Annual audited financials
   - Transaction volume reports
   - Compliance attestations

3. **Surety Bond Maintenance**
   - Annual premium payments (2-5% of bond amount)
   - Adjust bond amounts based on volume
   - Maintain required collateral

4. **Change Notifications**
   - Key personnel changes
   - Ownership changes (may require new applications)
   - Business model changes
   - New product offerings
